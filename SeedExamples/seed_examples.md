# Seed Examples for BWF Coaching Assistant Dataset

This file contains example prompt-response pairs representing the style, tone, and format expected in the final dataset.

---

## Example 1

**System**: You are a professional badminton coaching assistant. Provide accurate, structured, and technical information based on the BWF Coaching Framework and manuals.

**User**: What are the core areas covered in the BWF Coach Level 1 syllabus?

**Assistant**: The BWF Coach Level 1 syllabus covers the following core areas:
1. **How to Coach**: Basic coaching principles, communication, organization, and coaching methodologies suitable for beginners.
2. **What to Coach**: Basic technical, tactical, physical, and psychological elements of badminton.
3. **Planning and Delivery**: How to plan and deliver progressive and safe coaching sessions, including para-badminton.
4. **Group Management**: Safe management and organization of group activities and exercises.

## Example 2

**System**: You are a professional badminton coaching assistant. Provide accurate, structured, and technical information based on the BWF Coaching Framework and manuals.

**User**: How do the entry requirements and course duration differ between BWF Coach Level 1 and Level 2?

**Assistant**: Under the BWF Coaching Framework, the entry requirements and course durations differ as follows:
- **BWF Coach Level 1**:
  - **Entry Requirements**: Open to new/novice coaches or individuals interested in starting their coaching journey.
  - **Duration**: Typically a minimum of five days of tutored activities, plus tasked and guided coaching practice and planning.
- **BWF Coach Level 2**:
  - **Entry Requirements**: Requires candidates to be certified Level 1 coaches or equivalent. It also offers a fast-track option for players with national or international badminton experience.
  - **Duration**: Typically a minimum of six days of tutored activities, along with tasked/guided coaching practice and annual planning.

## Example 3

**System**: You are a professional badminton coaching assistant. Provide accurate, structured, and technical information based on the BWF Coaching Framework and manuals.

**User**: Explain the focus of BWF Coach Level 3 compared to Level 2.

**Assistant**: The focuses of BWF Coach Level 3 and Level 2 are as follows:
- **BWF Coach Level 2**: Focuses on the planning and delivery of annual training and competition programmes for individual badminton players. It covers technical, tactical, physical, and psychological elements underpinned by sports science principles.
- **BWF Coach Level 3**: Focuses on player development pathways, youth development, and elite performance. The course is highly interactive and applied, consisting of discussion, debate, reflection on practice, and significant post-course follow-up work with a tutor applied to the coach's professional context.
