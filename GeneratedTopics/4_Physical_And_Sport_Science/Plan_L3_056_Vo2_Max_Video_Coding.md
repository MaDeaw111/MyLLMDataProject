# Plan L3: VO2 Max Testing and Video Coding & Tagging Correlation

## Focus Summary
This elite plan correlates physiological load (VO2 Max Testing - VO2 max progressive shuttle tests and elite aerobic threshold workouts) with tactical video analytics (Video Coding & Tagging - match video tagging, coding key tactical rallies, and performance analysis). Coaches use heart rate telemetry and video tagging to identify if metabolic fatigue causes tactical errors and biomechanical decay in matches.

## Structured Drill Schedule

| Drill Phase | Duration | Feed Type | Execution Guidelines |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Biomechanical Analysis & Video Setup** | 20 Minutes | Video / Shadow | Run biomechanical sequences, checking joint angles and movement symmetry.<br>• Wear heart rate telemetry bands during high-intensity tactical rallies.<br>• Tag matches in real-time to link fatigue states to error rates. |
| **2. High-Load Target Drill** | 30 Minutes | Machine / Racket Feed | High-velocity cooperative feeds. Player executes target strokes under fatigue. |

## Sports Science & Research Integration
According to match analysis software data, tactical errors spike when heart rates exceed 95% HRmax due to cognitive narrowing. Applying cold-water immersion (CWI) and contrast bath hydrotherapy post-session accelerates metabolic waste clearance, keeping athlete cognitive reaction times below 220ms.

---

## Target Metrics for Improvement

* **Fatigue-Error Threshold**: Identify individual heart rate zone where tactical error rate increases by >20%.
* **Lactate Recovery Rate**: Blood lactate clearance optimized to <2.0 mmol/L within 15 minutes of hydrotherapy.
* **Reaction Accuracy**: Retain video coding decision accuracy >90% during fatigue stress.

## Core Purpose & Objectives (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร)

The core purpose of mastering **Plan L3: VO2 Max Testing and Video Coding & Tagging Correlation** (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร) is to build the physical capabilities and physiological resilience required for high-performance badminton. Developing energy systems (aerobic/anaerobic endurance), explosive strength, speed-agility, and flexibility is critical. Incorporating sports medicine, hydration, PHV load monitoring, and active recovery prevents overtraining and sports injuries.

## Deep-Dive Synthesis

Physiological adaptation in badminton requires targeting **Drill Phase**. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) activates the anaerobic lactic system, building tolerance to lactate accumulation. Concurrently, aerobic endurance training supports rapid recovery between rallies and matches.

To prevent overtraining, managing the workload, as discussed in **Biomechanical Analysis & Video Setup**, is critical. Tracking heart rate metrics and using subjective fatigue scales help coaches adjust training volume. Under PHV growth spurts, volume must be carefully scaled to protect growing bones and joints.

Furthermore, combining rehabilitation protocols with **High-Load Target Drill** ensures safe return-to-play. Eccentric strengthening for ankle and shoulder joints stimulates tissue remodeling. Hydration loading and carbohydrate-caffeine protocols also play a vital role in preventing dehydration-induced fatigue.

## Practical Coaching Application

The coach runs a badminton-specific conditioning circuit consisting of 40-second high-intensity multi-shuttle movements followed by 20 seconds of rest. This drill targets **Drill Phase** (e.g. anaerobic lactic tolerance). The coach monitors players' heart rates during the session. If a player's recovery heart rate does not drop below 130 bpm during rest, the coach scales down the feeding speed to ensure safety and prevent overtraining.
