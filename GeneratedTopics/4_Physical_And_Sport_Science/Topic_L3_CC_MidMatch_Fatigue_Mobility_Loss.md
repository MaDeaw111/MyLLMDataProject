High-intensity interval training (HIIT) activates the anaerobic lactic system, building tolerance to lactate accumulation. Concurrently, aerobic endurance training supports rapid recovery between rallies and matches.

To prevent overtraining, managing the workload, as discussed in **Using deceptive drops and clears to move opponents without sprinting.**, is critical. Tracking heart rate metrics and using subjective fatigue scales help coaches adjust training volume. Under PHV growth spurts, volume must be carefully scaled to protect growing bones and joints.

Hydration loading and carbohydrate-caffeine protocols also play a vital role in preventing dehydration-induced fatigue.
