# Plan L1: Maturation - Growth Spurt Management via Shaping Method

## Focus Summary
This training plan targets junior players undergoing biological maturation (Growth spurt maturation tracking and balance retraining) using Shaping Method. Coaching maturing youth requires tracking Peak Height Velocity (PHV) and adjusting training loads to prevent injuries. This lesson adapts training volume and uses progressive shaping from shadows to feed drills to address coordination changes ('adolescent awkwardness').

Shadow movement calibration** | 20 Mins | Shadow (Self-Fed) | Player runs shadow sequences focusing on Growth Spurt Management biomechanics and ready posture.<br>• Keep movement distances short to protect growing joints.<br>• Use lighter equipment (short rackets) and slow shuttlecocks.<br>• Maintain low impact loads, focusing on dynamic coordination. |

## Sports Science & Research Integration
BWF maturation studies show that junior players during growth spurts experience temporary changes in their center of mass, affecting timing and agility. Task constraint scaling (under-11 court size reductions) reduces tibial G-force impact loads to <4.0 G, mitigating risk of patellar tendinopathy while retaining high motor learning rates.

---

## Target Metrics for Improvement

* **Technical Consistency**: 80% of strikes must land inside the target court markers.
* **Movement Recovery**: Player returns to center base under 1.5 seconds after hit.
* **Split-Step Timing**: Pre-tension landing timed within 100ms of feed impact.
* **Maturation Load limit**: Under PHV growth spurts, continuous high-impact landing must be restricted to <15 minutes.

## Core Purpose & Objectives (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร)

The core purpose of mastering **Plan L1: Maturation - Growth Spurt Management via Shaping Method** (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร) is to build the physical capabilities and physiological resilience required for high-performance badminton. Developing energy systems (aerobic/anaerobic endurance), explosive strength, speed-agility, and flexibility is critical. Incorporating sports medicine, hydration, PHV load monitoring, and active recovery prevents overtraining and sports injuries.

## Deep-Dive Synthesis

Physiological adaptation in badminton requires targeting **Shadow movement calibration**. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) activates the anaerobic lactic system, building tolerance to lactate accumulation. Concurrently, aerobic endurance training supports rapid recovery between rallies and matches.

To prevent overtraining, managing the workload, as discussed in **Target feed sequence**, is critical. Tracking heart rate metrics and using subjective fatigue scales help coaches adjust training volume. Under PHV growth spurts, volume must be carefully scaled to protect growing bones and joints.

Furthermore, combining rehabilitation protocols with **Continuous match simulation** ensures safe return-to-play. Eccentric strengthening for ankle and shoulder joints stimulates tissue remodeling. Hydration loading and carbohydrate-caffeine protocols also play a vital role in preventing dehydration-induced fatigue.

## Practical Coaching Application

The coach runs a badminton-specific conditioning circuit consisting of 40-second high-intensity multi-shuttle movements followed by 20 seconds of rest. This drill targets **Shadow movement calibration** (e.g. anaerobic lactic tolerance). The coach monitors players' heart rates during the session. If a player's recovery heart rate does not drop below 130 bpm during rest, the coach scales down the feeding speed to ensure safety and prevent overtraining.
