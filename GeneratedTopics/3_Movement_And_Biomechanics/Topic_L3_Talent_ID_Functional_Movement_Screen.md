# Functional Movement Screening in Junior Academies

## Structural Outline

1. Screening overhead deep squat mechanics to check ankle/hip mobility.
2. Identifying unilateral imbalances in single-leg landing control.

## Core Purpose & Objectives (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร)

The core purpose of mastering **Functional Movement Screening in Junior Academies** (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร) is to optimize movement efficiency and stroke biomechanics. Establishing correct footwork (chasse, crossover), split-step timing, and kinetic chain coordination allows players to cover the court quickly and strike from a stable base. This minimizes energy expenditure, improves reaction speed, and prevents chronic mechanical stress on the lower limbs.

## Deep-Dive Synthesis

Understanding the biomechanical parameters of **Screening overhead deep squat mechanics to check ankle/hip mobility.** is essential for movement efficiency. The split-step mechanism must be timed perfectly with the opponent's strike, pre-tensioning the calf muscles to allow explosive lateral or forward acceleration. Incorrect split-step timing increases reaction time significantly.

During court travel, managing decelerating forces, as seen in **Identifying unilateral imbalances in single-leg landing control.**, protects joints from high impact. The heel-to-toe lunge landing, with the knee aligned directly over the ankle, prevents patellar tendinopathy. Coaches must focus on correct foot alignment during rapid direction changes.

Finally, the kinetic chain sequence of **Tracking thoracic spine rotation symmetry to prevent compensation injuries.** dictates power output. Force generated from leg extension and hip rotation must transfer smoothly through the thoracic spine and shoulder girdle. Smooth energy transfer ensures high racket head speed without overloading the rotator cuff muscles.

## Practical Coaching Application

In a movement session, the coach sets up a shadow footwork circuit. Cones are placed at the four corners. Players perform a split-step at the center base and push off to a corner, focusing on **Screening overhead deep squat mechanics to check ankle/hip mobility.** (e.g. heel-to-toe lunge landing). The coach uses a high-speed camera to record the movements, showing players their knee alignment and ankle angles during rest intervals to correct bad landing habits immediately.
