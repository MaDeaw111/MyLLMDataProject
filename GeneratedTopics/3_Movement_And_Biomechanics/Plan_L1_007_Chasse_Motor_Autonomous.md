# Plan L1: Chasse Footwork and Autonomous Stage Learning Integration

## Focus Summary
This training plan focuses on the movement mechanics of Chasse Footwork (Chasse side-sliding movements for court coverage) integrated with Autonomous Stage Learning. For Level 1 coaches, establishing footwork pathways is critical. Using autonomous stage random feeding and match-play simulation, the coach guides the player through movement cycles (split-step, push-off, travel, hit, recovery) to ensure biomechanical efficiency and joint alignment.

## Sports Science & Research Integration
Kinematic tracking of footwork cycles reveals that split-step pre-tension reduces reaction latency by up to 100ms. This session utilizes Autonomous Stage Learning to build correct muscle pre-activation, ensuring the player maintains balance during rapid change of direction on court, preventing chronic footwork instabilities.

---

## Target Metrics for Improvement

* **Technical Consistency**: 80% of strikes must land inside the target court markers.
* **Movement Recovery**: Player returns to center base under 1.5 seconds after hit.
* **Split-Step Timing**: Pre-tension landing timed within 100ms of feed impact.
* **Maturation Load limit**: Under PHV growth spurts, continuous high-impact landing must be restricted to <15 minutes.

## Core Purpose & Objectives (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร)

The core purpose of mastering **Plan L1: Chasse Footwork and Autonomous Stage Learning Integration** (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร) is to optimize movement efficiency and stroke biomechanics. Establishing correct footwork (chasse, crossover), split-step timing, and kinetic chain coordination allows players to cover the court quickly and strike from a stable base. This minimizes energy expenditure, improves reaction speed, and prevents chronic mechanical stress on the lower limbs.

## Deep-Dive Synthesis

Understanding the biomechanical parameters of **Shadow movement calibration** is essential for movement efficiency. The split-step mechanism must be timed perfectly with the opponent's strike, pre-tensioning the calf muscles to allow explosive lateral or forward acceleration. Incorrect split-step timing increases reaction time significantly.

During court travel, managing decelerating forces, as seen in **Target feed sequence**, protects joints from high impact. The heel-to-toe lunge landing, with the knee aligned directly over the ankle, prevents patellar tendinopathy. Coaches must focus on correct foot alignment during rapid direction changes.

Finally, the kinetic chain sequence of **Continuous match simulation** dictates power output. Force generated from leg extension and hip rotation must transfer smoothly through the thoracic spine and shoulder girdle. Smooth energy transfer ensures high racket head speed without overloading the rotator cuff muscles.

## Practical Coaching Application

In a movement session, the coach sets up a shadow footwork circuit. Cones are placed at the four corners. Players perform a split-step at the center base and push off to a corner, focusing on **Shadow movement calibration** (e.g. heel-to-toe lunge landing). The coach uses a high-speed camera to record the movements, showing players their knee alignment and ankle angles during rest intervals to correct bad landing habits immediately.
