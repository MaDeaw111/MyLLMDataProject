During court travel, managing decelerating forces, as seen in **Evaluating thoracic reach capacity during late shuttle contacts.**, protects joints from high impact. The heel-to-toe lunge landing, with the knee aligned directly over the ankle, prevents patellar tendinopathy. Coaches must focus on correct foot alignment during rapid direction changes.

Finally, the kinetic chain sequence of **Strengthening core obliques to support off-balance rotation.** dictates power output. Force generated from leg extension and hip rotation must transfer smoothly through the thoracic spine and shoulder girdle. Smooth energy transfer ensures high racket head speed without overloading the rotator cuff muscles.
