# Plan L2: Singles Four Corners with Partner Communication

## Focus Summary
This session focuses on the psychological skills of Partner Communication (verbal and non-verbal cues for doubles pairing synchronization) during Singles Four Corners tactical plays. Under physiological load, anxiety can narrow visual search search fields. Level 2 coaching integrates mental skills training to build self-talk and attentional focus under high score pressure.

---

## Structured Drill Schedule

| Drill Name | Duration / Volume | Feed Type | Execution & Technical Guidelines |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Dynamic Agility Intervals** | 30s work / 60s rest<br>3 sets of 6 reps | High-Speed Multi-Feed | Feeder plays rapid random shots. Player recovers to base. Focus on push-offs.<br>• Apply motivational cues or somatic breathing before serving.<br>• Gaze anchored solely on the opponent's racket contact point.<br>• Practice doubles pairing verbal callouts. |
| **2. Tactical Rotation Loop** | 20 Minutes | Cooperative Partner Play | Partners practice offensive rotations, driving and covering the seams. |
| **3. Pressure Game Simulation** | 25 Minutes | Live 2-on-1 Feeder Play | Player defends against two attackers, using clears and blocks to escape pressure. |

## Sports Science & Research Integration
David Alder's visual search research shows that somatic anxiety and physical fatigue narrow the player's attentional field, degrading reaction times to flick serves. This protocol applies Partner Communication techniques to maintain gaze control, keeping response latency under 250ms even during high heart rate zones (Zone 4/5: 170-190 bpm).

---

## Target Metrics for Improvement

* **Heart Rate Zone**: Maintain Zone 4/5 (170-190 bpm) during work, dropping below 130 bpm during rest intervals.
* **Lactate Tolerance**: Peak lactate accumulation between 8.0-12.0 mmol/L to trigger upregulation of MCT1/MCT4 transporters.
* **Neuromuscular Recovery**: Restrict vertical jump height velocity loss to <10% post-session.
* **Response Latency**: Maintain reaction time to flick serves and blocks under 250ms under fatigue.

## Core Purpose & Objectives (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร)

The core purpose of mastering **Plan L2: Singles Four Corners with Partner Communication** (ทำไปเพื่ออะไร) is to develop tactical intelligence and match play strategies. Coaches must train players to understand court coverage, doubles rotations, and singles patterns to exploit opponent weaknesses. This ensures players can make rapid decisions, dictate the rally's tempo, and execute tactical plans under competitive pressure.

## Deep-Dive Synthesis

Analyzing **Dynamic Agility Intervals** highlights the importance of spatial awareness and court coverage. In singles or doubles match play, players must execute patterns that create empty spaces in the opponent's court. This requires consistent variation in shuttle speed and angle to force the opponent out of their recovery base.

Under match stress, managing transitions, as detailed in **Tactical Rotation Loop**, dictates who controls the rally. Defensive block-to-counter transitions or offensive net-kill follow-ups must be executed automatically. Coaches should use representative match play constraints to build situational awareness in training.

Additionally, coordinating tactics with **Pressure Game Simulation** is key to sustaining performance. Analyzing opponent tendencies and adjusting server/receiver positioning during intervals can shift match momentum. Coaches should use video analysis tools to map tactical consistency during competitive cycles.

## Practical Coaching Application

The coach sets up a half-court singles game where Player A is constrained to play only drops and clears, while Player B can play any stroke but must target the backhand corner. This tactical scenario highlights **Dynamic Agility Intervals**. The coach stands at the side, noting how players adjust their court coverage and base position. During the interval, the coach reviews tactical sheets to show players how their recovery positions affect the rally outcome.
